# PhD Thesis — Writing Guide

## What Distinguishes a PhD Thesis from a Master Thesis

A PhD thesis must demonstrate an **original contribution to knowledge**. This is the
single most important criterion. The candidate must show that they have advanced the
field, not merely applied existing methods competently. The thesis must convince the
reader (and the examiners) that the work is novel, significant, and rigorous.

## The "Red Thread" — Narrative Arc Across Chapters

A PhD thesis is not a collection of papers stapled together. Even when based on
published work, it must have a coherent narrative arc — a "red thread" that connects
every chapter. The Introduction poses the overarching question. Each results chapter
addresses a piece of that question. The Discussion weaves the pieces together into
a unified answer. The reader should never wonder "why is this chapter here?"

## Chapter Structure and Flow

1. **Abstract** — 300-600 words. Must convey the original contribution clearly.
2. **Introduction** — Broad motivation, problem statement, research questions,
   objectives, and thesis outline. Sets up the overarching narrative.
3. **Literature Review** — Comprehensive, critical survey. Must go beyond the master
   level: identify not just the gap but the theoretical context for the contribution.
4. **Theoretical Framework** — Required. Present the conceptual or mathematical
   framework that underpins the thesis. This may draw on existing theory or propose
   new theoretical constructs.
5. **Methodology** — Detailed and justified. For a PhD, this chapter should also
   discuss methodological contributions if any (new techniques, adaptations, etc.).
6. **Results** — May span 2-4 chapters, one per study, experiment, or major analysis.
   Each results chapter should be self-contained with its own mini-introduction,
   methods specifics, results, and discussion.
7. **Discussion** — Synthesizes across ALL results chapters. This is where the "red
   thread" comes together. Discusses implications for the field, connections between
   studies, and how the work advances theory.
8. **Conclusions** — Summary of contributions (numbered list recommended). Explicit
   statement of original contributions.
9. **Future Work** — Must be substantive, not a throwaway paragraph. Propose specific
   studies, explain why they matter, and outline feasible approaches.

## How to Frame Original Contributions

- State contributions explicitly in both the Introduction and Conclusions.
- Use clear language: "This thesis contributes..." or "The original contributions
  of this work are..."
- Number the contributions (Contribution 1, 2, 3...) for clarity.
- Each contribution should be testable or falsifiable — avoid vague claims like
  "improved understanding."

## Actionable Instructions for the LLM

- The Theoretical Framework chapter is mandatory and must not be merged into the
  Literature Review. It should present the lens through which results are interpreted.
- Results chapters should each follow a mini-paper structure: context, specific
  methods, results, preliminary discussion.
- The overarching Discussion chapter must synthesize across results chapters, not
  merely repeat their individual discussions.
- Future Work must contain at least 3 specific, feasible research directions with
  brief rationale for each.
- Target length: 150-300 pages. Results chapters collectively should be 40-50% of
  the thesis.
- Each chapter must begin with an overview paragraph and end with a summary that
  transitions to the next chapter.
- The narrative arc must be explicit: Introduction poses the question, each chapter
  advances toward the answer, Conclusions deliver it.
