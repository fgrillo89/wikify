# Master Thesis — Writing Guide

## What Makes a Good Master Thesis

A master thesis demonstrates that the student has achieved mastery of research methods
in their field. Unlike a PhD thesis, it does not need to present a wholly original
contribution to knowledge, but it must show the ability to independently design,
execute, and analyze a research project with rigor.

## Chapter Structure and Flow

1. **Abstract** — Concise summary (250-500 words). Problem, methods, key results,
   and conclusion.
2. **Introduction** — Motivation, problem statement, research questions or hypotheses,
   scope, and thesis outline. The reader should know exactly what to expect.
3. **Literature Review** — Comprehensive and critical, not just a summary of papers.
   Must identify the gap that this thesis addresses. Organize thematically, not
   chronologically. Demonstrate command of the field.
4. **Methodology** — Justify every methodological choice. Explain not just what was
   done but why this approach was selected over alternatives. Include enough detail
   for replication.
5. **Results** — Present findings clearly with appropriate tables and figures. May be
   combined with Discussion if the narrative benefits from it.
6. **Discussion** — Interpret results in the context of the literature review. What do
   the findings mean? How do they relate to prior work? Address limitations honestly.
7. **Conclusions** — Summarize contributions, state limitations, and suggest future
   work. Must clearly articulate what this thesis adds to the field.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Literature review too long, analysis too short.** The literature review should
  support the thesis, not dominate it. A 40-page lit review with a 10-page results
  chapter signals imbalance.
- **Methodology without justification.** "We used method X" is insufficient. Explain
  why X and not Y. What are the tradeoffs?
- **Vague conclusions.** "More research is needed" is not a contribution statement.
  Be specific about what was learned and what remains.
- **Disconnected chapters.** Each chapter should explicitly connect to the previous
  one. Use transition paragraphs.

## Actionable Instructions for the LLM

- Literature Review must synthesize across sources, not summarize paper-by-paper.
  It must end with a clear identification of the gap this thesis fills.
- Methodology must include a justification subsection for each major methodological
  choice (why this method, these parameters, this sample size).
- Results and Discussion may be combined or separate — follow the convention of the
  field. If combined, still clearly distinguish findings from interpretation.
- Include a Limitations subsection (in Discussion or Conclusions). Address at least
  3 specific limitations and their impact on the findings.
- Target length: 80-150 pages. Literature Review should be roughly 20-25% of the
  total, Results + Discussion roughly 40-50%.
- Each chapter should open with a brief paragraph stating its purpose and close with
  a transition to the next chapter.
