# Research Article — Writing Guide

## Exemplary Models

Standard IMRaD (Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion) papers published in
high-impact journals like Nature, Science, JACS, or field-specific top venues. The
structure is rigid by convention and reviewers expect strict adherence.

## Structure

1. **Abstract** — Four moves: context, gap, approach + results, significance. One
   paragraph, no citations.
2. **Introduction** — Funnel from broad context to specific gap. End with a clear
   statement: "Here we show/report/demonstrate..."
3. **Methods** — Past tense, enough detail for reproduction. Include specific
   parameters, equipment models, software versions.
4. **Results** — Present findings in logical order (not chronological order of
   experiments). Lead each subsection with its main finding.
5. **Discussion** — Compare with prior work. Propose mechanisms. Acknowledge
   limitations honestly. State implications.
6. **Conclusion** — 1-2 paragraphs. Primary contribution plus specific future work.

## Key Conventions

- **Abstract**: Should stand alone. A reader who only reads the abstract should know
  what was done, what was found, and why it matters.
- **Results vs. Discussion**: Results states what happened; Discussion explains why.
  Keep them separate unless the journal explicitly allows combined sections.
- **Figures**: Every figure must be referenced in text. "Figure N shows..." followed
  by interpretation, not just description.
- **Limitations**: Address them proactively in the Discussion. Reviewers will find
  them — better to acknowledge them yourself.

## Actionable Instructions for the LLM

- Introduction must end with a clear hypothesis or objective statement.
- Methods must use past tense and passive or active voice consistently.
- Each Results subsection should open with the key finding, then present supporting
  data.
- Discussion should explicitly connect findings back to the gap stated in the
  Introduction.
- Do not introduce new results in the Discussion section.
- Conclusion should state what was achieved and 1-2 concrete next steps.
