# Academic Writing Style Guide

**Sentences:** Active voice. Characters as subjects. Kill nominalizations. One concept per sentence. One relative clause max. Specific numbers not vague quantifiers. Short words (use/show/about not utilize/demonstrate/approximately).

**Paragraphs:** One point, topic sentence first, 4-8 sentences. Known-new contract: familiar first, new last.

**Citations:** Weave into narrative. Author names for key findings; brackets for background. Never fabricate.

**Claims:** Every claim needs a citation, data, or derivation. Hedge superlatives. Distinguish observation from inference. Precise comparisons need referent + magnitude + conditions.

**Banned words:** delve, crucial, pivotal, paramount, groundbreaking, cutting-edge, novel (use "new"), innovative, landscape/tapestry/beacon/realm (metaphorical), multifaceted, "it is important to note," "In recent years" (opener), showcases/underscores/highlights (use shows/supports/indicates), foster, leverage, harness.

**Hard bans:**
- ZERO em-dashes as parenthetical separators. Use commas or parentheses.
- No bullets in paper body (Methods only).
- No meta-commentary: the document is never the grammatical subject.
- No methodology disclosure: never reveal search strategy or source count.
- No structural scaffolding visible to the reader (no **Known:** / **Missing:** labels).
- Consistent terminology throughout.

**Abstract:** (1) accessible context, (2) key observation, (3) evidence, (4) reader takeaway. First sentence <=15 words. No citations. No jargon before definition.

**Tone:** Confident expert, first person ("we"). No grandiosity, no false modesty.

**Title:** Specific, 10-15 words, <100 chars, no marketing language.

## Banned Phrases (project-wide, all page kinds)

The following phrases are forbidden in any generated wiki page body. They
are corpus meta-commentary -- they describe the evidence source rather than
the subject, and they expose the generation process to the reader.

- "in this corpus"
- "appears in this corpus"
- "mentioned in this corpus only through citations"
- "this corpus contains"
- "in this article"
- "as discussed above"

First-person references to the work are also banned: "we examine",
"we show", "our analysis", and any equivalent phrasing that implies the
writer is a participant rather than an encyclopedic narrator.
