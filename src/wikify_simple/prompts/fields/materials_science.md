# Materials Science Field Guide

## Exemplary Papers
- George (2010) "Atomic Layer Deposition: An Overview" — Chem. Rev. ALD review with clear precursor and growth-rate chemistry.
- Strukov et al. (2008) "The Missing Memristor Found" — Nature. HfO2 and TiO2 memristive switching paper; a model of how a thin-film device paper opens with a clear physical mechanism.
- Wong et al. (2012) "Metal Oxide RRAM" — Proc. IEEE. RRAM survey: filament physics, electrode chemistry, and array integration tied together.
- Yu (2018) "Neuro-Inspired Computing with Emerging Nonvolatile Memory" — Proc. IEEE. Neuromorphic memristor crossbar review.
- Robertson (2006) "High-K Dielectrics for VLSI" — Eur. Phys. J. Appl. Phys. HfO2 dielectric properties: structure, composition, electrical characterization.

## Field Conventions
- **Structure**: Introduction (application gap), Experimental/Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion. Process parameters reported in full.
- **Citations**: Numbered ACS or author-year Elsevier. Primary sources preferred over reviews.
- **Figures**: XRD patterns, SEM/TEM micrographs, XPS survey scans, IV curves, retention/endurance plots. Scale bars and axis labels required. Multi-panel figures consistently labeled.
- **Tone**: Quantitative, instrument-aware, precise. Active voice in results, passive voice in methods. Avoid puffery.
- **Units**: SI throughout. Thickness in nm, temperature in K or C (pick one), pressure in Pa or Torr (pick one).

## Actionable Instructions
- Open with the application need, not the history of the technique.
- Report all process parameters (temperature, pressure, precursor chemistry, pulse and purge times, substrate, ALD or sputtering or PLD).
- Characterize in this order: structure (XRD, TEM), then composition (XPS, EDS), then properties (electrical, optical, mechanical).
- Compare quantitatively against prior work, with citation, exact numbers, and matching conditions.
- Captions self-contained: name the material, the technique, and the key observation.
- Link processing to microstructure to properties explicitly.
- Report sample counts, standard deviations, and batch-to-batch variation.
- Quote film thicknesses with the technique used to measure them.
