# Atomic layer deposition overview

Atomic layer deposition is a thin-film deposition technique based on the sequential use of self-limiting gas-phase chemical reactions. Tuomo Suntola pioneered the method in the 1970s for thin-film electroluminescent displays.

## Process cycle

Each atomic layer deposition cycle consists of two half-reactions separated by inert gas purges. In the first half-reaction the precursor adsorbs on the substrate surface and saturates the available reactive sites. The purge removes excess precursor before the second precursor is introduced and reacts with the adsorbed surface species.

## Applications

Atomic layer deposition produces conformal films on high aspect ratio substrates with sub-nanometer thickness control. This makes it the technique of choice for gate dielectrics in logic transistors, diffusion barriers in interconnects, and protective coatings on battery electrodes. The method is also used to deposit catalyst overlayers on porous supports, where conformality and thickness uniformity are critical for catalytic performance.
