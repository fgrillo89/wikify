# Heterogeneous catalysis principles

Heterogeneous catalysis describes catalytic reactions in which the catalyst occupies a different phase from the reactants, most often a solid catalyst acting on gas or liquid reactants. Gerhard Ertl received the Nobel prize in chemistry for elucidating the surface chemistry of ammonia synthesis on iron.

## Surface adsorption

Reactants must first adsorb onto the catalyst surface. Adsorption may be physical, governed by van der Waals forces, or chemical, involving the formation of true chemical bonds with surface atoms. Chemisorption is usually a prerequisite for heterogeneous catalytic activity.

## Active sites

Catalytic activity is concentrated at specific surface configurations called active sites. These may be step edges, kinks, defects, or particular crystallographic facets. Identifying and controlling active sites is a central goal of modern catalyst design, and atomic layer deposition is one tool used to engineer such sites with precision.
