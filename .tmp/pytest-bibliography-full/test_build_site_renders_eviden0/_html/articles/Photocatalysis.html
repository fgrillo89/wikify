Definition

Photocatalysis is the acceleration of a chemical reaction by light.

Mechanism / Process

Photocatalysis proceeds through electron-hole pair generation under illumination1.

As shown in Figure 1, the band-gap alignment drives the reaction1.

Charge carriers migrate to the surface and drive redox chemistry1. The mechanism is supported by Atomic Layer Deposition coatings1.

Key Facts

  • Photocatalysis is widely used in water splitting1.
  • It depends on the band gap of the semiconductor1.
  • Quantum efficiency is the standard metric1.

In This Corpus

The corpus emphasises photocatalysis as a route to solar fuels1. It is discussed in multiple primary sources1.
