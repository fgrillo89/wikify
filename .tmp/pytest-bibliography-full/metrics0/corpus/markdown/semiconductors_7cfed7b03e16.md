# Semiconductor physics primer

A semiconductor is a material whose electrical conductivity falls between that of a conductor and an insulator. Pure silicon and germanium are the canonical examples. Walter Brattain and his colleagues at Bell Labs demonstrated the first transistor using a germanium crystal in 1947.

## Band structure

The electronic states of a crystalline semiconductor form continuous bands separated by an energy gap called the band gap. The valence band is fully occupied at zero temperature; the conduction band is empty. Thermal excitation, photon absorption, or doping moves electrons across the gap and produces mobile charge carriers.

## Doping

Substituting a small fraction of host atoms with dopants having a different valence introduces either extra electrons or extra holes. Doping concentrations and dopant species determine the carrier density and the position of the Fermi level, and so control nearly every electronic property of the semiconductor.
